Macroeconomic Resilience: Strategic Analysis of Global Markets for 2025

By Dr. Julian V. Sterling • Senior Economist • February 2025

As we enter the first quarter of 2025, the global financial landscape is characterized by a "Great Realignment." Central banks, having battled the inflationary spikes of previous years, are now pivoting toward growth-stabilization policies. This transition period presents both unprecedented risks and unique structural opportunities for institutional and retail market participants alike.

Key Insight: The correlation between traditional fiat currencies and decentralized assets has reached an all-time high.

1. Monetary Policy and Interest Rate Volatility

The Federal Reserve and the ECB have signaled a departure from aggressive tightening. However, the "Higher for Longer" mantra has left a lasting impact on credit markets.
